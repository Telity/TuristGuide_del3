Need to know about the Little Mermaid

The Little Mermaid (Danish: Den lille Havfrue) is a bronze statue by Edvard Eriksen, depicting a mermaid becoming human. The sculpture is displayed on a rock by the waterside at the Langelinie promenade in Copenhagen, Denmark. It is 1.25 metres (4.1 ft) tall and weighs 175 kilograms (385 lb). Based on the 1837 fairy tale of the same name by Danish author Hans Christian Andersen, the small and unimposing statue is a Copenhagen icon and has been a major tourist attraction since its unveiling in 1913. In recent decades it has become a popular target for defacement by vandals and political activists.

Mermaid is among iconic statues that symbolize cities; others include: the statue of Pania of the Reef in Napier, Manneken Pis in Brussels, the Statue of Liberty in New York, Christ the Redeemer in Rio de Janeiro, or Smok Wawelski (Wawel Dragon) in Kraków, Poland.

Fun facts

  • Inspired by a Fairy Tale : Based on Hans Christian Andersen's famous story
  • Unveiled in 1913 : Located in Copenhagen, Denmark
  • Small Size : Only 1.25 meters (4.1 feet) tall
  • Bronze Statue : Made of bronze and granite
  • Donated : Gifted by Danish brewer Carl Jacobsen
  • Vandalism : Frequently targeted, including beheadings and paint attacks
  • Tourist Attraction : One of Copenhagen's most visited landmarks
